# Domain: article

Use for writing, outlining, restructuring, or understanding an article, essay, report, newsletter, or long-form narrative.

## Required coverage

| Coverage key | Required content |
| --- | --- |
| `article.purpose` | What the writing should change, explain, persuade, or enable. |
| `article.audience` | Target reader, prior knowledge, motivation, and likely obstacle. |
| `article.thesis` | Core claim or final understanding in one specific statement. |
| `article.narrative` | Reading path and why each transition moves the argument forward. |
| `article.sections` | Section sequence and the distinct argumentative job of each section. |
| `article.evidence` | Required arguments, examples, data, cases, stories, or citations and their role. |
| `article.objections` | Counterarguments, reader doubts, or comprehension barriers and responses. |
| `article.openingClosing` | How the opening establishes the problem and the ending resolves it or asks for action. |
| `article.gaps` | Missing material, unresolved claims, and fact-checking needs. |

## Source rules

When organizing existing material, distinguish source fact, author opinion, quotation, and AI interpretation. Never invent a quote, attribution, date, link, study, or statistic.
