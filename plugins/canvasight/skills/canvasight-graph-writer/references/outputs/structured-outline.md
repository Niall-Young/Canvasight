# Output: structured-outline

Use a left-to-right hierarchy for articles, definitions, reviews, and material that has a reading order.

- One root establishes purpose or thesis.
- Section nodes state their distinct responsibility; child nodes contain evidence, examples, questions, or details.
- Split a section when its parts can stand as independently readable sections; do not split supporting sentences that only make sense as one argument.
- Avoid identical depth for every branch when the material does not support it.
- Place the root at the left and advance sections toward the right. Arrange sections or supporting material at the same dependency level as parallel branches rather than stacking them into a vertical chain.
- Preserve source order through explicit ordering in node titles or bodies and through edges only where one section truly depends on the previous one. Reading order alone does not justify connecting every section into one chain.
